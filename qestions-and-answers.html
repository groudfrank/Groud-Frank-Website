What's your calling in life?

I don't think my calling is anything career related. My calling in life is to be a decent human being not to be a developer or a fireman or a farmer or a priest. Don't get me wrong, there is wrong with passionately choosing a career. Our personalities and temparaments generally point us to certain fields and our jobs can change people's lives for the better but the kind of change we affect comes from who we are and not necessarily what we do.

What do you want your legacy to be?

I'm honestly not very concerned with leaving a legacy. The universe is approximately 13.7 billion years old and the earth is appromixmately 4.5 billion. Humans have been here for approximately 200,000 years and I am 28 years old. Given the scale of the universe(size and age) it's kinda pointless trying to leave a legacy behind. I think we like holding on to our mortality so much that we subconciously try to be immortal by leaving things behind. We are afraid people will forget us so we try to leave a mark. The problem there is that we are attempting to leave a mark on the earth but that's only going to matter for as long as the earth and people are around and both those things are finite - as any scientist or priest will tell you. They may disagree about how it will come to an end but they agree on the end result. I want my legacy to be obedience to God. If I aim for that I may be pleasing in the eyes of God and may, in the process, leave a legacy behind but if I aim for leaving a legacy on earth I may achieve neither of those things.
